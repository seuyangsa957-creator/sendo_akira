① 1-Year ROIIC is mechanically distorted in acquisition years: the full purchase price hits invested capital immediately (day of close) while NOPAT from the acquired business builds over several years. This is why 1-year figures should not be used to assess M&A quality for a serial acquirer like Stryker. The 3- and 5-year rolling windows allow NOPAT time to ramp before drawing a conclusion on return.

Interpretation: The 5-year rolling ROIIC reaching 26.4% in 2025 — its highest point in the 14-year series — suggests Stryker's capital deployment has improved in quality over time. Earlier acquisitions (2013–2016 wave) generated lower incremental returns as integration dragged NOPAT. The more recent cohort (Wright Medical, Vocera, Inari) appears to be maturing into higher-returning assets.

Methodology: ROIIC = (NOPATt − NOPATt-n) / (ICt − ICt-n) where n = 1, 3, or 5 years. Uses same NOPAT and IC as Version 1 ROIC. Source: Stryker 10-K, S&P Capital IQ. Author's calculations.
